Food

Switzerland is known for their chocolate and fondue, but here are underrated dishes from Switzerland that locals love.

Basler Läckerli

The Basler Läckerli (also Leckerli or Läggerli, lecker meaning "delicious" in German and -li being a diminutive suffix) is a traditional hard spice biscuit originating from Basel, Switzerland. It has been around for over 700 years. These biscuits are made with honey, hazelnuts, almonds, candied fruit peel, and a bit of Kirsch. After baking, the warm dough gets coated with a sweet sugar glaze and is cut into small rectangular pieces.

Bündner Birnbrot

Birnbrot or Birnenbrot (German) translates to “Pear bread.” They're enjoyed all across Switzerland, and many regions have their own versions. Some well-known ones include Bündener Birnbrot from Graubünden, Glarner Birnbrot from Glarus, Toggenburger Birnbrot from Toggenburg, and Luzerner Birnweggen from Lucerne.

Gâteau du Vully

A delicious yeasted cake featuring puits d'amour or “wells of love.” The most famous version of this cake is made with sugar and cream, but there's also a savory version that includes bacon and cumin.
